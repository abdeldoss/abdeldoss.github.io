About IP Addresses

An IP (Internet Protocol) address is a unique identifier assigned to each device connected to a network. Your public IP address is how websites and online services identify your network on the internet.

IPv4 vs IPv6

IPv4 addresses are 32-bit (e.g., 192.168.1.1) while IPv6 are 128-bit (e.g., 2001:0db8:85a3::8a2e:0370:7334) to accommodate more devices.

Dynamic vs Static

Most home users have dynamic IPs that change periodically, while businesses often use static IPs that remain constant.
